Machine Learning using Neural Network & KNN

The team developed a machine learning model to predict if the song will be a hit based on certain attributes of the song. At first we tried to predict the exact popularity of the song but the models accuracy was very low, hence the output nodes were decreased to just two, will the song be a hit or a miss? (i.e. Will the song end up in top 10 of the billboard 100?)


Activation Input nodes Hidden nodes Output nodes Epochs Accuracy (test data) Deep layer Accuracy (test data)
reLu 13 39 2 100 67.7% 1 67.8%
sigmoid 13 39 2 100 67.7% 1 68.1%
linear 13 39 2 100 65.6% 1 65.6%
tanh 13 39 2 100 67.9% 1 67.6%
tanh 13 130 2 100 67.8% 1 67.5%
tanh 13 130 2 1000 67.7% 1 62.5%

The activation functions reLu, sigmoid, linear, and tanh all produced similar Accuracy results. And though we added a deep layer, the Accuracy did not change much.

Activation Input nodes Hidden nodes Output nodes Epochs Accuracy (test data) Deep layer Accuracy (test data)
reLu 13 39 92 100 8.3% 1 8.3%
reLu 13 39 10 100 33.1% 1 32.9%
reLu 13 39 2 100 67.7% 1 67.8%

As we adjusted the output nodes from 92 to 10 to 2 using reLu, we found that the Accuracy % increased due to the relatively limited output nodes.

Top Song Bin Input nodes Hidden nodes Output nodes Epochs Accuracy (test data) Deep layer Accuracy (test data)
10 13 39 2 100 67.9% 1 67.6%
15 13 39 2 100 61.7% 1 61.3%
25 13 39 2 100 67.7% 1 65.9%

We did not observe a change in the Accuracy upon increasing the number of songs in the top song bin.


We also built a KNN classifier model that resulted in a similar accuracy of 67.4% as our NN model. Considering both NN and KNN models generated similar accuracy levels, we believe our NN model is robust and can be enhanced by adding more data or training it with a more comprehensive data set.
